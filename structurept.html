How to Make Papeda

 

  1. 100 grams of sago flour
  2. 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  3. 300 ml of hot boiling water
  4. A mixing bowl
  5. A wooden spoon or spatula

 

  1. Put the sago flour into a mixing bowl.
  2. Add the salt and stir it evenly.
  3. Carefully pour the boiling water little by little into the bowl.
  4. Stir the mixture quickly using a wooden spoon.
  5. Continue stirring until the mixture becomes sticky and transparent like glue.
  6. Once it thickens, your papeda is ready to be served.

 

Serving Suggestion:

Papeda is usually eaten with yellow fish soup (ikan kuah kuning) or spicy tuna. Use chopsticks or a wooden fork to twist the papeda before dipping it into the soup.

How to Wire Money Online

 

First, you’ll need to make sure you’ve signed up for internet

banking with your bank. With most banks you have to actually

request this be set up – just having a bank account doesn’t mean you

automatically have internet banking access. You will need to call or

visit your bank to get this facility set up.

 

Once you have an internet banking account, log in to your

online interface. You’ll usually see a summary of accounts, along with

a button that gives you an option to transfer money. This is the option

you want to click on.

 

You should now see a screen which allows you to choose

which of your accounts you want to send money from, and enter

the details of who you want to send it to. Select the account where

you have the funds, and enter the details of the recipient to send the

funds to.

 

Once all details are filled out you should see a ‘Confirm’ or

‘Make payment’ button – click on this and finalize your transfer. The

funds will then be processed.

 

Source: Bahasa Inggris: English for Change untuk SMA/MA Kelas X
